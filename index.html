About Our Program

A Clinic Built Around Families, Not Just Patients

The Rosen Familial Endocrine Genetics Clinic at MedStar Georgetown University Hospital is a dedicated program for the identification, evaluation, and long-term management of individuals and families with hereditary endocrine tumor syndromes.

Led by Dr. Jennifer Rosen, our clinic integrates advanced next-generation sequencing with compassionate, individualized genetic counseling. We work closely with endocrinology, surgery, and oncology teams to ensure every patient receives seamlessly coordinated care.

Because hereditary endocrine conditions affect entire families, our program extends its scope to at-risk relatives — offering proactive screening and surveillance before tumors develop. Early identification saves lives.


Genetic Risk Assessment

Thorough review of personal and family history to determine hereditary risk for endocrine tumors, with tailored recommendations for genetic testing.

Multigene Panel Testing

We offer comprehensive 24-gene hereditary endocrine cancer panels using next-generation sequencing, detecting single nucleotide and copy number variants.

Cascade Family Screening

Once a pathogenic variant is identified, we coordinate targeted testing for first- and second-degree relatives, enabling early detection and prevention.

Multidisciplinary Coordination

Our genetic counselors work hand-in-hand with endocrinologists, surgeons, and oncologists to deliver unified, evidence-based care plans for each patient.

Are You at Risk? Consider Genetic Counseling.

You may benefit from our program if you or a family member has a history of thyroid cancer, parathyroid disease, pituitary tumors, pheochromocytoma, paraganglioma, or pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors — or if a pathogenic variant has already been identified in your family.

Step-by-Step Process

How Our Screening Program Works

From your first appointment to long-term family surveillance, every step is designed with transparency and precision.

1
Intake

Referral & Initial Intake

Patients are referred by their primary care physician, endocrinologist, oncologist, or surgeon — or may self-refer. Accepted indications include:

  • Personal history of medullary thyroid cancer, pheochromocytoma, paraganglioma, or parathyroid disease
  • Known pathogenic variant in an endocrine tumor gene within the family
  • Two or more first-degree relatives with endocrine tumors
  • Early-onset endocrine tumor (<40 years of age)
  • Multiple primary endocrine tumor diagnoses in a single individual
2
Consultation

Comprehensive Genetics Consultation

Your first appointment is with one of our certified genetic counselors and, when appropriate, Dr. Rosen. We review a detailed three-generation pedigree, evaluate clinical history, discuss inheritance patterns, and address psychosocial concerns.

3
Testing

Multigene Panel Genetic Testing

Next-generation sequencing analyzes up to 24 genes via blood or saliva sample. Results in 2–4 weeks. Detects SNVs and CNVs, classified per ACMG/AMP guidelines. Insurance pre-authorization assistance available.

4
Results

Results Disclosure & Interpretation

Results are disclosed in a dedicated follow-up appointment — never by letter alone. Our counselors walk through each result in plain language, covering implications for health and next steps.

5
Management

Surveillance & Management Planning

Personalized surveillance plan aligned with NCCN and Endocrine Society guidelines, including biochemical panels, imaging (MRI pituitary, abdominal CT/MRI, thyroid ultrasound), and surgical consultation when indicated.

6
Family

Cascade Family Screening

We coordinate testing for at-risk first- and second-degree relatives, facilitate single-site testing, and enroll family members into our longitudinal surveillance program.

Research Programs

Our Areas of Scientific Focus

Our translational research program bridges laboratory discovery and patient care, with ongoing studies across six principal domains.

🧬

Genotype–Phenotype Correlation in MEN1

Characterizing the relationship between specific MEN1 variant types and tumor penetrance, onset age, and aggressiveness across 200+ families in our registry to inform variant-specific surveillance intervals.

🔬

VUS Reclassification & Functional Assays

Cell-based assays characterizing pathogenicity of VUS in MEN1, CDC73, and CDKN1B, in collaboration with Georgetown's functional genomics laboratory.

📊

Outcomes After Prophylactic Thyroidectomy in RET Carriers

Retrospective study examining surgical timing and long-term recurrence in pediatric and adult RET variant carriers, with focus on ATA risk category concordance.

👨‍👩‍👧

Psychosocial Impact of Cascade Genetic Testing

Studying psychological burden, family communication patterns, and decisional regret among first-degree relatives who undergo cascade testing — in collaboration with Georgetown's Department of Psychiatry.

🏥

Disparities in Hereditary Endocrine Cancer Diagnosis

Retrospective analysis examining racial, ethnic, and socioeconomic disparities in referral rates and access to multigene panel testing across the MedStar Health system.

⚗️

Novel Biomarkers in Hereditary Pheochromocytoma

Evaluating plasma and urine metabolomics in SDH-mutation carriers to identify early biomarkers of tumor development prior to imaging detection, with the NIH Intramural Program.
